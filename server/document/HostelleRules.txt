Hostel Rules and Regulations

Introduction

The University Hostel is committed to providing a safe, secure, and comfortable living 
environment for all residents. The hostel serves as an extension of the academic 
community, encouraging discipline, mutual respect, and personal responsibility. 
Every resident is expected to follow the hostel rules and cooperate with the 
administration to maintain a peaceful atmosphere. These regulations are designed to 
ensure the well-being, safety, and convenience of all students residing in the hostel.

Admission and Accommodation

Hostel accommodation is allotted based on eligibility criteria established by the 
institution. Room allocation is carried out by the Hostel Office and may be changed 
only with prior approval from the Hostel Warden. Students are responsible for 
maintaining the room and furniture provided to them throughout the academic year. 
Unauthorized exchange of rooms or sharing accommodation with non-residents is strictly 
prohibited. Residents are required to verify the condition of hostel property at the 
time of check-in and report any existing damages immediately.

Hostel Timings

The hostel follows fixed entry and exit timings to ensure student safety and campus 
security. Residents are expected to return to the hostel before the designated closing 
time unless prior permission has been granted by the Hostel Warden. Students leaving 
the hostel for personal work or vacations should inform the hostel office and complete 
the necessary formalities. Repeated violations of hostel timings may result in 
disciplinary action or restriction of hostel privileges.

Attendance Policy

Daily attendance is maintained to ensure the safety and accountability of all residents.
Students are expected to be present during attendance hours unless they have received 
official approval for leave. Attendance records are maintained by the hostel 
administration and may be reviewed periodically. Continuous absence without 
permission may lead to disciplinary proceedings and may affect eligibility for 
continued hostel accommodation.

Room Maintenance and Cleanliness

Residents are individually responsible for maintaining cleanliness inside their rooms. 
Rooms should be kept hygienic and organized at all times. Waste should be disposed of 
only in designated dustbins provided on each floor. Students should avoid writing on 
walls, damaging furniture, or making structural modifications to hostel rooms. 
Periodic inspections may be conducted by hostel authorities to ensure compliance with 
cleanliness and maintenance standards.

Electrical Appliances

To ensure electrical safety and prevent fire hazards, only approved electrical 
appliances are permitted inside hostel rooms. Devices such as laptops, mobile chargers,
 and study lamps may be used responsibly. High-power appliances including electric 
 heaters, induction stoves, microwave ovens, electric kettles, refrigerators, and 
 immersion rods are not permitted unless specifically authorized by the hostel 
 administration. Unauthorized appliances may be confiscated during inspections.

Internet and Network Usage

High-speed wireless internet is available throughout the hostel premises to support 
academic activities and research. Students are expected to use the network responsibly 
and comply with institutional information technology policies. Activities involving 
illegal downloads, hacking attempts, copyright violations, or excessive bandwidth 
consumption are strictly prohibited. Network privileges may be suspended for misuse or 
activities that compromise system security.

Visitor Regulations

Visitors are permitted only during officially designated visiting hours and must 
register at the security desk before entering the hostel premises. Visitors are 
allowed only in common visitor areas and are not permitted inside student rooms 
without prior approval. Overnight accommodation for visitors is not allowed under 
any circumstances. Residents are responsible for the conduct of their visitors while 
they remain within the hostel premises.

Safety and Security

The hostel places high priority on the safety of its residents. Students should always 
carry their hostel identity card while entering or leaving the premises. Emergency 
exits, fire extinguishers, smoke detectors, and other safety equipment should not be 
tampered with under any circumstances. Residents should immediately report suspicious 
activities, unauthorized persons, theft, or security concerns to the hostel office or 
security personnel. Cooperation with security staff during inspections and emergency 
situations is mandatory.

Leave and Vacation Procedures

Students intending to leave the hostel for overnight stays, vacations, or extended 
periods must submit a leave request through the prescribed procedure before departure. 
Leave applications should include travel details, destination, duration, and emergency 
contact information. Permission must be obtained from the Hostel Warden before leaving 
the campus. Students returning after the approved leave period should immediately 
report to the hostel office.

Mess and Dining Services

The hostel mess provides nutritious and hygienic meals according to a fixed daily 
schedule. Students are expected to maintain discipline while dining and avoid wasting 
food. Dining utensils and furniture should be handled carefully and returned to their 
designated places after use. Outside catering or cooking inside hostel rooms is 
discouraged unless permitted by the administration. Suggestions regarding food quality 
or menu improvements may be submitted through the hostel feedback system.

Discipline and Student Conduct

Residents are expected to conduct themselves in a respectful and responsible manner at 
all times. Activities that disturb other students, including excessive noise, arguments,
 or inappropriate behavior, are strictly prohibited. Respect for fellow residents, 
 hostel staff, and institutional property is essential for maintaining a positive living
  environment. Students should comply with instructions issued by hostel authorities 
  and cooperate during inspections, maintenance activities, and emergency situations.

Prohibited Activities

The hostel maintains a strict zero-tolerance policy toward activities that threaten 
the safety or well-being of residents. Possession or consumption of alcohol, tobacco 
products, narcotic substances, and other prohibited materials is strictly forbidden 
within the hostel premises. Gambling, ragging, physical violence, harassment, vandalism,
 possession of weapons, and any form of illegal activity will attract severe 
 disciplinary action. Violations may result in suspension, cancellation of hostel 
 accommodation, or legal proceedings depending on the seriousness of the offense.

Maintenance and Repair Services

The hostel administration provides regular maintenance services to ensure that hostel 
facilities remain functional and comfortable. Students should promptly report issues 
related to electricity, plumbing, internet connectivity, furniture, or sanitation 
through the hostel maintenance portal or office. Residents should not attempt to 
repair electrical or mechanical equipment on their own. Maintenance requests are 
generally processed according to their urgency and available resources.

Emergency Procedures

In the event of fire, natural disasters, medical emergencies, or other unforeseen 
incidents, residents should remain calm and follow the instructions provided by hostel 
authorities and security personnel. Emergency evacuation routes are displayed on every 
floor and should be familiar to all students. Fire exits must remain unobstructed at 
all times. Medical emergencies should be reported immediately to the hostel office, 
campus medical center, or emergency response team to ensure timely assistance.

Conclusion

The hostel rules are intended to create a safe, disciplined, and supportive 
environment where students can focus on their academic and personal development. 
Every resident shares the responsibility of maintaining cleanliness, respecting 
fellow students, protecting hostel property, and following institutional policies. 
The hostel administration reserves the right to amend these regulations whenever 
necessary, and residents are expected to stay informed about any updates issued 
during the academic year.
